The Story of Vikram and Vetal

Once upon a time, there was a brave king named Vikramaditya. He was known far and wide for his wisdom and courage.

One day, a sage asked King Vikram to capture a Vetal, a spirit that haunted a nearby forest. The sage explained that the Vetal was tricky and would often narrate a story and then ask a riddle.

Vikram agreed and went to the forest. After a long and dangerous journey, he managed to capture the Vetal. As they were returning, the Vetal started narrating a story:

"Once there were three friends, each with a unique talent. The first could run as fast as the wind, the second could fly like a bird, and the third could swim like a fish. They decided to have a race. Who won?"

Vikram pondered for a moment and replied, "The one who can fly like a bird won because they could start on land, fly through the air, and finish in the water."

As soon as Vikram answered the riddle, the Vetal escaped and returned to the forest.

This happened many times, with the Vetal escaping after each story and riddle. Each time, Vikram had to capture it again. The stories were full of wisdom and lessons.

And so, King Vikram continued his quest to capture the Vetal, and the cycle of stories and riddles continued, with each story teaching valuable lessons.
